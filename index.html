The Unconference

The first rOpenSci unconference in Australia will be held on Thursday and Friday (April 21-22) in sunny Brisbane, at the Microsoft Innovation Centre. This event will bring together researchers, developers, data scientists and open data enthusiasts from industry, government and university. We aim to conceptualise and develop R-based tools that address current challenges in data science, open science and reproducibility.

Proceedings will follow the rOpenSci unconference model. This means there is a focus on doing, there are no talks and no presentations. Problems to be tackled are generated collaboratively by participants in advance via GitHub. Past projects have related to open data, data visualization, data publication and open science using R. To ensure a safe, enjoyable, and friendly experience for everyone who participates, we have a code of conduct. We hope this will be the first event of many that fosters a connected, engaged and vibrant Australian R and open science community.

The Venue

We will be hosting the first Australian Hackathon at the Microsoft Innovation Centre in Brisbane. This space puts us high into the Brisbane skyline; an excellent space for solving problems. It also has some capacity for breaking into smaller groups.

Schedule of events

We will tease out the hackathon ideas using GitHub issues on the hackathon repo a few weeks before the event. Some examples of past projects include testdat, geojsonio, and guides for reproducibility. Keeping in line with previous rOpenSci unconferences, the schedule of the event will not be decided until the morning of the 21st of April

Join us

Deadline is April 1st, 2016

Nominations closed April 1st 2016.

The Hackathon works best as a small event with 30-40 attendees. A selection committee is evaluating nominations to pick a combination of people that are representative of a variety of career stages, fields, genders, and locations.

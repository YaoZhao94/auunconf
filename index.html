The Unconference

For the first time ever, there will be an rOpenSci hackathon in Australia! The event will be held on Thursday and Friday (April 21-22) in sunny Brisbane, at the Microsoft Innovation Centre, with some welcome drinks on the Wednesday evening for those that can attend. We're organizing this event to bring together scientists, developers, and open data enthusiasts from academia, industry, government, and non-profit organisations to get together for a few days and hack on various projects. Past projects have related to open data, data visualization, data publication and open science using R. To ensure a safe, enjoyable, and friendly experience for everyone who participates, we have a code of conduct.

The Venue

We will be hosting the first Australian Hackathon at the Microsoft Innovation Centre in Brisbane. This space puts us high into the Brisbane skyline; an excellent space for solving problems. It also has some capacity for breaking into smaller groups.

Schedule of events

We will tease out the hackathon ideas using GitHub issues on the hackathon repo a few weeks before the event. Some examples of past projects include testdat, geojsonio, and guides for reproducibility. Keeping in line with previous rOpenSci unconferences, the schedule of the event will not be decided until the morning of the 21st of April.

Join us

Deadline is April 1st, 2016

Please nominate yourself if you'd like to attend.

The Hackathon works best as a small event with 30-40 attendees. A selection committee will evaluate nominations to pick a combination of people that are representative of a variety of career stages, fields, genders, and locations.
